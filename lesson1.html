Lesson 1 Plan — Design in Context

Product analysis as the entry point for A Level D&T. A 60-minute live online session that establishes the analytical habits students will use throughout the course.

Duration60 minutes

Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson students will be able to:

  • Apply the five-lens analytical framework to any designed product
  • Use correct A level vocabulary: user needs, material properties, manufacturing process, sustainability, cultural context
  • Distinguish between GCSE-level description and A level analysis and justification
  • Understand the scope and expectations of the course

Online tip: Use the annotation tool to draw directly onto the product image — circle material junctions, point to form language, highlight ergonomic features. This models the annotated thinking students need in their NEA portfolios.

The Five-Lens Framework

This framework runs through the entire A level. Introduce it explicitly in Lesson 1 so students have a mental scaffold for every product they encounter — in the exam, in the NEA, and in everyday life.

Key Vocabulary

TermDefinition
User-centred designDesigning around the needs, wants and values of the end user
Material propertiesMechanical (strength, hardness), physical (density, conductivity), aesthetic (colour, texture, finish)
Iterative designThe process of repeated design, prototype, test, and refine cycles
Lifecycle assessment (LCA)Evaluating environmental impact from raw material extraction to disposal
Design movementA shared set of aesthetic and philosophical principles across a period (e.g. Bauhaus, Modernism)

Differentiation

Support

  • Provide a structured analysis template with sentence starters for each lens
  • Pair with a stronger student in breakout room during independent task
  • Pre-teach: send a short 3-min video clip on the product before the lesson

Extension

  • Compare two products from the shortlist — same lens, different products. Where do design decisions diverge and why?
  • Research the designer — how does their background and context shape the product?
  • Find a product that fails on one of the five lenses. Evaluate why it was still commercially successful.

Independent Task — Before Lesson 2

Find any product in your home. Photograph it. Then write a short analysis (one paragraph per lens) covering:

  1. User — who is it for? What needs does it meet?
  2. Materials — what is it made from and why?
  3. Manufacturing — how do you think it was made?
  4. Sustainability — what happens to it at end of life?
  5. Cultural context — when was this type of product invented? How has it changed?

Upload your photo and analysis to the shared class folder before Lesson 2. We will use these as warm-up discussion material.

Assessment for Learning

No formal assessment in Lesson 1. Use the chat and live discussion to gauge:

  • Are students moving beyond description to explanation? ("It is made of plastic" vs "Polypropylene is used because its impact resistance suits a product likely to be dropped")
  • Are they linking material choice to user need? To manufacturing process?
  • Do they understand what "analyse" and "evaluate" mean at A level?
If students are still describing rather than analysing after the guided section, pause and explicitly model the difference: read a description aloud, then rephrase it as analysis. This is a habit they need to build from day one.
