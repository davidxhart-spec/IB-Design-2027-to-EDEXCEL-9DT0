1
Design Thinking & Process
Weeks 1–5 · Teach first
The analytical and iterative habits that underpin everything else. Establishing these early means every subsequent cluster is taught with the right mindset already in place.
IB DT 2027
  • Design cycle: Inquire → Develop → Create → Evaluate
  • Criterion A: Inquiring & Analysing — context, need, design brief
  • Criterion B: Developing Ideas — design concepts, iterative development
  • Criterion D: Evaluating — testing, reflection, improvement
  • Innovation, invention, design opportunity identification
  • Primary & secondary research methods
IB strength: depth of inquiry before design. Students already think cyclically.
Edexcel 9DT0
  • Topic 9: Iterative design — Explore, Create, Evaluate
  • User-centred design, design frameworks, problem framing
  • Topic 12: Strategies to explore, create & evaluate design ideas
  • Component 2: NEA — student-led iterative project
  • Product analysis as analytical method (Component 1, all years)
  • Research methods for NEA context identification
Edexcel: same cycle, different name. "Explore → Create → Evaluate" maps 1:1 to IB design cycle.
85%
content overlap
Teach once
One set of lessons. IB students frame as Criterion A/D. Edexcel students frame as Topic 9 + NEA scaffold. Same activities, different write-up language.
2
Sustainability & Ethics
Weeks 4–8 · Teach second
Highest content overlap of all six clusters. Teach early so sustainability thinking threads through everything — materials selection, manufacturing, product analysis, NEA briefs.
IB DT 2027
  • Criterion D: Evaluating — environmental & social impact assessment
  • Lifecycle thinking: extraction → manufacture → use → disposal
  • Circular economy, cradle-to-cradle design
  • Ethical sourcing, fair trade, labour conditions
  • Planned obsolescence vs durability
  • Social responsibility in design contexts
  • UN Sustainable Development Goals as design framework
IB goes deeper here than Edexcel — SDGs, systemic thinking. This is a teaching advantage; IB content enriches Edexcel lessons.
Edexcel 9DT0
  • Topic 4: Sustainability & Social Responsibility (full topic)
  • LCA — lifecycle assessment methodology
  • Circular economy principles
  • Carbon footprint, energy consumption in manufacture
  • Planned obsolescence, design for disassembly
  • Ethical sourcing, Fairtrade, supply chain transparency
  • 6 Rs: Recycle, Reuse, Reduce, Repair, Refuse, Rethink
Edexcel: Topic 4 is one of the highest-yielding exam topics. Extended 6–12 mark Qs appear every year. Worth sustained teaching time.
90%
content overlap
Teach once
Strongest shared cluster. One set of resources works for both. IB students apply to Criterion D evaluation. Edexcel students use same content for Topic 4 exam Qs.
3
People & Society
Weeks 6–10 · Third
Human factors, cultural context, design movements and the relationship between design and society. Underpins product analysis in both qualifications. Strong overlap but Edexcel requires named movements and dates — IB does not.
IB DT 2027
  • Human factors: ergonomics, anthropometrics, universal design
  • Inclusive design — designing for diverse users
  • Cultural, social & economic influences on design
  • Innovation in social context — disruptive vs incremental
  • Design and globalisation — access, equity, appropriateness
  • Ethics of design — whose needs are prioritised?
IB explores this philosophically — the "why" of design's relationship to society. Rich discussion material.
Edexcel 9DT0
  • Topic 1: Design in context — movements, influential designers, history
  • Topic 9: Ergonomics, anthropometrics, user-centred design
  • Topic 11: Business context — market needs, IP, design economy
  • Named movements required: Bauhaus, Arts & Crafts, Modernism, Postmodernism, Memphis Group, Sustainable Design
  • Influential designers: Rams, Eames, Starck, Newson, Hadid
  • Social responsibility: designing for developing contexts
Edexcel: named movements & designers are tested explicitly. This is where IB teachers need to add factual depth to their existing conceptual understanding.
80%
content overlap
Adapt framing
Teach the conceptual core together. Add a dedicated Edexcel session on movements and designers. IB students can attend — enrichment, not duplication.
4
Materials
Weeks 8–16 · Fourth
Edexcel requires systematic knowledge by material family. IB teaches materials contextually. Strong conceptual overlap, but Edexcel students need additional recall depth. Teach conceptual core together, then Edexcel-only systematic sessions.
IB DT 2027
  • Material properties: mechanical, physical, aesthetic
  • Smart materials, composites, technical textiles
  • Material selection criteria — fitness for purpose
  • Sustainability of materials — ethical sourcing, recyclability
  • Emerging materials: graphene, aerogels, self-healing polymers
  • Material testing methods — destructive & non-destructive
IB approaches materials through design context — "why this material for this brief?" rather than systematic family-by-family coverage.
Edexcel 9DT0
  • Topic 2: Papers & boards, polymers, metals & alloys, timber, textiles, composites
  • Topic 3: Smart materials, technical textiles, modern materials
  • Properties: mechanical, physical, aesthetic — by family
  • Surface treatments, finishes — by material family
  • Material selection: fitness for purpose + sustainability
  • Testing: hardness, tensile strength, toughness, malleability
Edexcel: students must know properties for ALL families systematically. Calculation Qs on material properties appear in Component 1 most years.
65%
content overlap
Adapt framing
Teach concepts together. Run 4–5 Edexcel-only systematic sessions on material families. IB students can use these as enrichment — deeper recall supports their NEA/IA material justification.
5
Manufacturing & Digital Technologies
Weeks 14–24 · Fifth
Manufacturing knowledge supports both NEA/IA making decisions and Component 1 exam Qs. IB students apply this in their design portfolio. Edexcel requires greater commercial process detail and calculation fluency.
IB DT 2027
  • Criterion C: Creating the Solution — making, manufacturing decisions
  • Manufacturing processes chosen to suit material & brief
  • CAD/CAM: used in design development & prototype production
  • Digital fabrication: 3D printing, laser cutting, CNC
  • Prototype types: appearance, functional, scale models
  • Quality control in making — precision, tolerances
IB: manufacturing is applied through making — students choose and justify processes within their IA context. Less recall of commercial processes required.
Edexcel 9DT0
  • Topics 7–8: Wasting, forming, joining, casting, moulding — by process type
  • Commercial production: CNC routing, injection moulding, die casting, press forming
  • Scale: one-off, batch, mass, continuous — costs & implications
  • Topic 10: CAD/CAM, 3D printing, laser cutting, VR/AR in design
  • Tolerances, jigs, fixtures, quality assurance methods
  • Calculation Qs: material costs, weight, scale of production economics
Edexcel: commercial manufacturing processes must be known by name, input/output, suitability, cost implications. Calculation questions are a common weak point.
60%
content overlap
Adapt framing
Digital fabrication and CAD/CAM: teach together. Commercial processes (injection moulding, die casting etc.) and calculations: Edexcel-only sessions. IB students benefit from attending but don't need recall depth.
6
Assessment Craft
Throughout — embed from week 1
The biggest divergence between the two qualifications. IB uses holistic band descriptors; Edexcel uses point-based mark schemes. Both need sustained practice. Run qual-specific assessment sessions alongside every cluster — not as a separate unit.
IB DT 2027
  • Criteria A–E: Inquiring, Developing, Creating, Evaluating, Presenting
  • Band descriptors: 0–6 per criterion, holistic judgement
  • IA portfolio: 35–50 pages, process-led, iterative evidence
  • Command terms: analyse, evaluate, justify, outline, describe, discuss
  • External exam: Paper 1 (SL) + Paper 2 (HL) — product analysis + extended response
  • Moderator reads portfolio as a narrative — process matters as much as outcome
IB: assessors reward genuine inquiry and honest evaluation. A well-argued imperfect prototype scores higher than a perfect one with shallow evaluation.
Edexcel 9DT0
  • Component 1: 120 marks — short answer, calculations, extended writing (Q8: 12 marks)
  • Mark scheme: each mark = one correct point or reasoning chain
  • Command words map to mark allocation: give (1mk), explain (2mk), evaluate (4–12mk)
  • Component 2 NEA: assessment grid, CAB completed by teacher
  • Marks awarded at grid level — evidence must be explicit and annotated
  • No reward for process not evidenced — portfolio must be explicit
Edexcel: mark scheme precision is learnable. Students who understand command words and mark allocation outperform regardless of knowledge level. Teach this early and repeatedly.
30%
content overlap
Separate sessions
Keep assessment practice separate. Mixing IB band descriptor thinking with Edexcel mark-scheme thinking actively confuses students. Run qual-specific assessment sessions every 3–4 weeks.
Practical teaching sequence — week-by-week logic
Weeks Cluster / Focus Shared lesson content IB-only activity Edexcel-only activity Assessment practice
1–3 Design Thinking Product analysis (five-lens framework). What is design? Iterative vs linear process. User-centred design intro. Map product to Criterion A. Begin IA context inquiry. Component 1 Q style intro. First past paper product Q. Mark scheme walk-through. IB: Criterion A exemplar. Edexcel: 4-mark explain Q.
4–5 Design Thinking Research methods. Identifying user needs. Writing a design brief / specification. Stakeholder analysis. Draft IA design brief. Primary research plan. NEA brief options workshop. Context selection exercise. IB: Brief + specification draft. Edexcel: NEA context mind-map.
4–6 Sustainability LCA deep dive. Circular economy. 6Rs. Planned obsolescence case studies. Ethical sourcing. Evaluate a product against SDGs (Criterion D practice). Topic 4 past paper Qs — 6-mark sustainability extended Q. IB: Criterion D band practice. Edexcel: 6-mark model answer.
6–8 People & Society Human factors, ergonomics, anthropometrics. Inclusive design. Designing for diverse users. Cultural impact analysis of a chosen product. Globalisation discussion. Named designers + movements timeline. Bauhaus to present. Short-answer Q practice. IB: cultural analysis written response. Edexcel: Topic 1 short Qs.
8–10 People & Society Design movements deep dive. Business context. IP, patents, trademarks. Design economy. Innovation case study — social impact of a disruptive design. Topic 11: business models, costing exercise. IP scenario Q. IB: innovation essay outline. Edexcel: Topic 11 Qs.
8–12 Materials Material properties conceptual framework. Smart & composite materials. Emerging materials. Testing methods. Material selection for IA brief. Justify material choice against criteria. Topics 2–3 systematic coverage: polymers, metals, timbers, textiles, papers/boards. IB: material justification in IA. Edexcel: material properties Qs.
12–16 Materials Finishes and surface treatments. Material failure. Selection in context. Material testing evidence for IA portfolio. Calculation Qs: material weight, cost, volume. Edexcel-specific drill. IB: material testing evidence. Edexcel: calculation practice set.
14–20 Manufacturing CAD/CAM, digital fabrication, 3D printing, laser cutting. Prototype types and purposes. CAD development of IA concepts. Prototype planning. Topics 7–8: commercial processes by name. Scale of production economics. IB: CAD development log. Edexcel: manufacturing process Qs.
20–26 Manufacturing Quality control. Tolerances. Jigs and fixtures. Manufacturing constraints in design decisions. Making phase — Criterion C evidence. Process diary. NEA making phase + photo log. Topic 10 digital technologies. IB: Criterion C making evidence. Edexcel: NEA portfolio review.
Throughout Assessment Craft — Band descriptor practice every 3–4 weeks. Criterion-by-criterion IA portfolio review. Peer moderation. Mark-scheme Qs every 3–4 weeks. Extended Q8 practice from Y13 start. ResultsPlus analysis. Keep strictly separate. Never mix in same session.
Where the syllabuses genuinely diverge — teach separately, always

IB DT 2027 — no Edexcel equivalent

  • Criterion E: Presenting — communication of the design portfolio as a whole narrative. Edexcel has no equivalent presentation criterion.
  • HL only: Innovation Project — extended product development task beyond the standard IA. Edexcel has no HL/SL distinction.
  • SDG framework — explicit use of UN Sustainable Development Goals as a design evaluation lens. Edexcel references sustainability but not SDGs.
  • Philosophical inquiry — IB rewards genuine questioning of design assumptions. Edexcel rewards justified factual claims.
  • Theory of Knowledge (TOK) links — design as a way of knowing. No Edexcel equivalent.

Edexcel 9DT0 — no IB equivalent

  • Named design movements + dates — Bauhaus (1919), Memphis Group (1981), etc. Tested directly in Component 1. IB references movements conceptually without requiring date recall.
  • Calculation questions — material weight, cost, tolerances, scale of production economics. IB does not require mathematical calculations in assessment.
  • Commercial manufacturing detail — injection moulding, die casting, press forming — by process name, input/output, cost. IB is process-aware but less prescriptive.
  • Q8 extended synoptic question — 12 marks, requires integration across all topics. No IB equivalent single question of this type.
  • Electronics & programmable devices (Topic 6) — circuits, Arduino, sensors. IB covers electronics but not at this systematic depth.
