For IB DT teachers moving into or co-teaching Edexcel A Level D&T online · Lesson 1: Design in Context & Product Analysis · April 2026

IB DT 2027 ↔ Edexcel 9DT0
For IB DT teachers: Your existing knowledge maps powerfully onto Edexcel Lesson 1. The product analysis habit, user-centred thinking, and sustainability lens you already teach are all here — the differences are in framing, terminology, and exam technique. This guide shows exactly where you stand and what needs adapting.
Part 1 — Core Comparison: Lesson 1 Content
Aspect
IB DT 2027
Edexcel 9DT0
IB Teacher: what to do
Lesson 1 purpose
IB
Build design thinking habits. Introduce the design cycle. Students explore context before any brief is set.
Edexcel
Establish analytical voice for exam. Product analysis as the entry point. Shift from GCSE description to A level justification.
Direct match
IB's context-first approach is the same instinct. Frame it for students as: "we analyse products to understand design decisions — then justify them."
Core skill taught
IB
Inquiry + analysis. Students question design decisions, consider stakeholders, evaluate against criteria A–E.
Edexcel
Analysis + justification. Students explain WHY design decisions were made, link materials → properties → user need → manufacturing.
Adapt framing
Replace "inquiry" language with "justification" language. Same thinking, different output. Edexcel wants chains of reasoning: material → property → user need → decision.
Product analysis framework
IB
No fixed framework. Students typically use: function, aesthetics, ergonomics, sustainability, cultural impact. Criteria A (Inquiry) drives the questions.
Edexcel
Five-lens framework: User · Materials · Manufacturing · Sustainability · Cultural Context. Maps directly to Component 1 topics 1–12.
Adapt structure
IB teachers already use most of these lenses informally. Formalise them as the five headings. This becomes students' exam scaffold — use it consistently from Lesson 1.
Sustainability lens
IB
Central to the 2027 spec. Criterion D covers sustainability explicitly. Students evaluate LCA, circular economy, ethical sourcing throughout the course.
Edexcel
Topic 4 (Sustainability & Social Responsibility). LCA, circular economy, planned obsolescence, carbon footprint. Appears in every past paper.
Direct match
Your IB sustainability depth is a strength here. Edexcel students often underperform on sustainability Qs. Your IB experience gives you richer content to draw from.
User-centred design
IB
Criterion A (Inquiring & Analysing) — student identifies needs of client/end user before any design work begins. Human factors thread runs through all criteria.
Edexcel
Topic 9: User-centred design, ergonomics, anthropometrics. Appears in extended Q8 (12-mark synoptic question) most years. Also core to Component 2 NEA.
Direct match
Same concept. IB calls it "client/end user identification." Edexcel calls it "user-centred design." Introduce both terms in Lesson 1 so students are bilingual across specs.
Cultural context
IB
Addressed through "innovation and design" content: design movements, cultural influences on design, globalisation. More implicit than explicit in assessment.
Edexcel
Topic 1: Design movements, influential designers, historical context. Often tested as a short-answer Q. Bauhaus, Modernism, Postmodernism, Memphis Group expected.
Needs focus
IB doesn't require named movements to the same depth. Edexcel does. In Lesson 1, explicitly name Bauhaus/Modernism when discussing the product. Build a "movements timeline" early.
Materials knowledge
IB
Materials are contextualised within briefs. Properties taught as needed. 2027 spec emphasises smart/composite materials and sustainability of material choice.
Edexcel
Topics 2–3: Explicit, systematic coverage of all material families. Students need to know specific properties, manufacturing compatibility, and selection criteria for all major families.
Deepen systematically
IB covers materials in context; Edexcel requires systematic recall. In Lesson 1, start the habit of stating why a material is chosen (property → application). Topics 2–3 lessons will build on this.
Assessment language
IB
Command terms: analyse, evaluate, justify, outline, describe. Criteria A–E with band descriptors. Holistic portfolio evidence.
Edexcel
Command words: give, state, describe, explain, analyse, evaluate, assess, justify, discuss. Mark-scheme driven. Each mark = one point/chain of reasoning.
Translate carefully
IB "evaluate" = broad, holistic. Edexcel "evaluate" = reach a judgement with evidence. In Lesson 1, show students a mark scheme so they see how marks are awarded — this is new territory vs IB.
Lesson 1 product choice
IB
IB teachers often open with a design brief stimulus. Product analysis is used to identify design opportunities and user needs.
Edexcel
Product analysis as standalone analytical exercise. Braun T3 recommended: clear design intent, Rams connection, all five lenses rich. No brief required.
Familiar territory
You already do product analysis in IB. The difference: in Edexcel, the analysis IS the outcome, not the starting point for a brief. Teach students to see analysis as an exam skill, not just a design tool.
Part 2 — Five-Lens Framework: IB DT Mapping

Where each Edexcel Lesson 1 lens sits in IB DT 2027 — so you can draw on existing knowledge confidently.

1. User

Who is it for? Needs, wants, values. Ergonomics. Inclusive design.

IB: Criterion A — Inquiring & Analysing. Human factors throughout.
Edexcel: Topics 1, 9

2. Materials

What material, why that material? Properties, selection criteria, finishes.

IB: Materials content across all criteria. Smart/composite in 2027 spec.
Edexcel: Topics 2, 3

3. Manufacturing

How made? Scale of production. CAD/CAM. Tolerances. Commercial processes.

IB: Criterion B — Developing Ideas. Manufacturing constraints in NEA.
Edexcel: Topics 7, 8, 10

4. Sustainability

LCA. Circular economy. Ethical sourcing. Planned obsolescence. End of life.

IB: Criterion D — Evaluating. Central to 2027 spec. Extensive content.
Edexcel: Topic 4

5. Cultural Context

Design movements. Historical moment. Social & economic forces. Designer intent.

IB: Innovation & Design content. Less formally assessed than Edexcel.
Edexcel: Topics 1, 11

Part 4 — IB Teacher Confidence Map for Lesson 1

Honest assessment of where an experienced IB DT teacher can teach confidently, where to adapt, and where to spend prep time.

✓ Teach with full confidence

  • Product analysis as a teaching method
  • User-centred design / human factors
  • Sustainability lens (LCA, circular economy)
  • Iterative / cyclical design process
  • Encouraging student justification of decisions
  • Live annotation / screen-share pedagogy
  • NEA / portfolio supervision

⚠ Adapt your approach

  • Exam technique: mark-scheme vs band descriptors
  • Systematic materials knowledge (by family, not just in context)
  • Command word precision (Edexcel "evaluate" ≠ IB "evaluate")
  • Scale of production terminology and detail
  • Framing analysis as an exam output, not just a design tool
  • Introducing the five-lens framework explicitly

✗ Spend prep time here

  • Named design movements & dates (Bauhaus, Memphis, etc.)
  • Edexcel-specific commercial manufacturing detail (Topics 7–8)
  • Calculation questions (material weight, cost, tolerances)
  • Component 1 extended Q8 format (12-mark synoptic)
  • ExamWizard / ResultsPlus tools (Edexcel-specific)
