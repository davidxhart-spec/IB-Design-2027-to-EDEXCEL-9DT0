1. Technology Setup

Video call software installed and tested
Install Zoom / Google Meet / Teams — whichever your teacher uses. Test your microphone and camera before the first lesson.
Stable internet connection confirmed
Minimum 10 Mbps download for screen-sharing. Use ethernet cable if possible. Have a mobile hotspot as backup.
Second screen or tablet (optional but helpful)
Useful for watching the teacher's screen while taking notes or sketching on a second device.
Headphones with microphone
Essential for clear audio in group discussions and breakout rooms.

2. Accounts & Software

Autodesk Fusion 360 — registered and installed
Free education licence. Go to autodesk.com/education and register with your school email. Do this first — licence approval can take 3–5 days.
Tinkercad account created
Free, browser-based. Go to tinkercad.com and sign up. Used for quick 3D modelling and circuit simulation.
Google account (for shared docs and Drive)
Your teacher will share a class Google Drive folder. Make sure you have a Google account and can access Google Docs and Forms.
Edexcel qualification page bookmarked
qualifications.pearson.com — search '9DT0'. Bookmark the A level D&T page. Past papers and mark schemes are here.

3. Stationery & Equipment

A4 sketchbook or plain paper pad
Sketching by hand is a core skill at A level. You will need to sketch products, mechanisms, and design ideas regularly.
Pencils (HB and 2B), fine-liner pen, ruler
For annotation sketches and technical drawing practice.
Scientific calculator
Required in the Component 1 exam. Material calculation questions appear most years.
Camera or phone with good camera
You will photograph your NEA prototype and making process throughout Y12–Y13. Good lighting matters — have a plan for this.

4. Course Documents to Read Before Lesson 1

Read the Course Overview document (provided by teacher)
Understand the two components, the 12 topics, and how the NEA works.
Skim the Scheme of Work (provided by teacher)
See the two-year journey. You don't need to understand everything yet — just get a sense of the shape of the course.
Look at one past Component 1 paper
Download from the Pearson site. Don't worry about answering — just look at the style and types of question. This is what we're working towards.
Read Dieter Rams' 10 Principles of Good Design
Search "Dieter Rams 10 principles Vitsoe". One page. Essential context for Lesson 1.

5. Before Lesson 1 — Mini Task

Choose any product in your home
Something you find interesting or use every day. Could be a chair, a kettle, packaging, a tool, a piece of clothing — anything designed.
Photograph it from 3 angles
Front, side, and a detail shot. Keep the photos — you'll refer to them in your first lesson.
Jot down 3 things you notice about its design
No right or wrong answers. Just observe. What materials? What shape decisions? Who do you think it's for?

Questions before Lesson 1? Contact your teacher via email or the class messaging channel.
